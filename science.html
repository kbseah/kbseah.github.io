About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Developmental Biology in Tübingen, Germany, where I work on ciliates in the group of Estienne Swart. My doctoral work was performed at the Max Planck Institute for Marine Microbiology in Bremen, Germany, in the Symbiosis Department headed by Prof Nicole Dubilier.

Research Interests

My research interests lie in protists (microbial eukaryotes) and microbial symbionts, and in applying bioinformatics to tackle problems in genomics, phylogenetics, and organismal biology.

The unusual ciliate Kentrophoros and its ectosymbiotic bacteria were the focus of my doctoral research. Kentrophoros are found in marine sediment (muds, fine sand) of coastal areas around the world. The bacterial symbionts are attached to the ciliate’s surface, and use chemical energy from the environment (sulfur-oxidizing chemoautotrophy) to build up biomass, which the ciliates harvest for food.
